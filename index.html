Our Category

Conifers are cone-bearing, mostly evergreen trees and shrubs known for their needle-like or scale-like leaves. They reproduce via cones, with male cones producing pollen and female cones containing seeds. These trees play vital roles in ecosystems, providing habitat, stabilizing soil, and serving as a valuable source of wood. Common examples include pines, spruces, and firs, prized for their economic and ornamental value.

Evergreen refers to plants, mostly trees and shrubs, that retain their foliage throughout the year, unlike deciduous plants that shed their leaves seasonally. These plants often have adaptations like needle-shaped leaves or thick waxy coatings to conserve water. Evergreens provide year-round color and texture to landscapes, offer shelter for wildlife, and can serve as windbreaks and privacy screens. They are popular in landscaping for their aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements. Examples include pine, cedar, and holly trees.

Deciduous trees and plants are those that shed their leaves annually in response to seasonal changes, typically during the fall or autumn. This shedding allows them to conserve energy and water during colder months and prepare for new growth in the spring. Deciduous forests showcase vibrant foliage colors in autumn before the leaves fall, creating a picturesque landscape. Examples of deciduous trees include oak, maple, and birch, which are widely appreciated for their beauty and ecological importance.

Japanese maples, scientifically known as Acer palmatum, are ornamental trees or shrubs native to Japan, Korea, and China. They are prized for their stunning foliage, which comes in various colors including red, green, purple, and variegated combinations. Japanese maples are popular in landscaping for their graceful, delicate leaves and attractive branching patterns, adding beauty and elegance to gardens and landscapes. They prefer partial shade and well-drained soil, making them ideal for adding color and interest to gardens with their vibrant hues and unique textures.

Discover our Story

Founded in 1998-1999 on Vancouver Island, Outback Nursery originated as a retreat from city living. Surrounded by dense forests, we nurtured plants and animals. Transitioning from Farmer's Market vending to serving wholesale and landscaping customers, we grew steadily. Through the incorporation of greenhouses and irrigation, our nursery prospered, transforming into a beloved community haven that honors the wonders of the natural world.
